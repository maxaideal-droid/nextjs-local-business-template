# Voice Guide

Read this before writing any blog post, service page, or customer-facing copy.

---

## Tone

Write like a knowledgeable professional talking to a peer, not an advertiser talking to a mark. You know your trade. You are not performing enthusiasm.

Honest > impressive. If something has a downside, say so. If the client should not hire you for a particular need, say that too.

---

## Sentence rhythm

- Short sentences default. Long sentences when the idea genuinely requires it.
- Vary length to control pace. Three short sentences in a row reads staccato. Four long ones loses the reader.
- Read it aloud. If you run out of breath before a period, break the sentence.

---

## Vocabulary

Use the plainest word that is accurate.

- Plain > formal: "use" not "utilize", "start" not "commence", "show" not "demonstrate"
- Specific > vague: "7 days" not "about a week", "$65" not "competitive pricing"
- Active > passive: "we come to you" not "service is provided at your location"

---

## Formatting rules

- No exclamation marks
- No emojis in body copy
- Bold for genuinely important phrases — not decoration
- Bullets for lists of 3 or more items; prose for 2 or fewer
- Subheadings every 200–300 words in long posts

---

## Tells that it's AI-written (delete anything that matches)

If your draft contains any of these, delete and rewrite:

- "unlock", "leverage", "seamless", "world-class", "cutting-edge", "state-of-the-art"
- "in today's fast-paced world", "in the digital age", "more important than ever"
- "comprehensive", "holistic", "robust", "game-changer", "transformative"
- Opening with "Are you looking for...?" or "Have you ever wondered...?"
- Ending with "Contact us today to get started on your journey."
- Any sentence that could appear unchanged on any competitor's website
- Hedging with "may", "might", "could", "potentially" when you know the answer
- Three-word adjective stacks: "innovative, results-driven solutions"

---

## Required voice tells

Every page must include at least one of:

1. A place where you tell the reader NOT to use your service (strongest trust signal)
2. A specific number from `references/stats.md`
3. A direct opinion, not hedged — backed by evidence from `references/opinions.md`

---

## Content rules

- Start with the answer. Add context after.
- Primary keyword in the first 100 words.
- One story per post max — from `references/stories.md`. Never invented.
- One strong opinion per post max — from `references/opinions.md`, backed by a real number.
- All statistics must match `references/stats.md` exactly. Never round.
