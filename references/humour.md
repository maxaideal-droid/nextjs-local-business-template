# How This Brand Handles Humour

Read before writing any copy with a light or self-aware tone.

---

## The baseline

This brand is not a comedy brand. Humour is used sparingly, as a pressure-release valve, not as the main event.

---

## What works

**Self-aware asides.** Acknowledging a conflict of interest or an obvious bias — and doing it before the reader does — builds trust. Example: "I do this for a living, so read this with appropriate skepticism."

**Dry understatement.** Describing something as unremarkable when it is clearly remarkable. Or describing something complicated in a flat, matter-of-fact sentence.

**Specificity.** The funniest version of any joke is the most specific version. "The sun is older than civilization and completely unbothered by what it does to your skin" is funnier than "the sun causes damage."

---

## What does not work

- Puns on the service name or trade
- Exclamation marks as a substitute for energy
- Self-deprecation that undermines credibility
- Anything that could read as mocking the client
- Pop culture references that date

---

## Placement

Humour goes in: intros, section transitions, the "when not to hire us" sections.

Humour does not go in: pricing, FAQs, legal disclaimers, safety-related content.

---

## The test

Read the line out loud. If it sounds like a copywriter trying to be funny, cut it. If it sounds like the owner saying something true in an unexpectedly dry way, keep it.
